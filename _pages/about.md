I am a **Quantitative Researcher** with a PhD in Brain and Cognitive Sciences (University of Trento, CIMeC / Italian Institute of Technology) and a visiting period at the University of Oxford's Wellcome Centre for Integrative Neuroimaging. I am currently completing an MSc in Data Science.

My work sits at the intersection of **experimentation, psychometrics, and applied machine learning**, with a focus on understanding and predicting human behavior at scale. I am transitioning to industry roles in **quantitative UX research, behavioral data science, and insights analytics**.

## What I do

- **Experimentation & causal inference** — experimental design, A/B-style testing, mixed-effects models, quasi-experimental methods.
- **Survey science & psychometrics** — large-scale survey validation (up to N ≈ 40,000), measurement invariance, CFA/EFA/IRT, behavioral segmentation.
- **Applied ML & NLP** — Python (pandas, scikit-learn, statsmodels), R, text classification pipelines, LLM benchmarking, explainable AI.
- **Mixed methods** — semi-structured interviews, moderated user sessions, qualitative synthesis integrated with quantitative signal.

## Selected achievements

- **First-author publication** in the *Journal of Cognitive Neuroscience* (Tosi et al., 2026).
- Validated a 42-item behavioral instrument on **N = 39,775** respondents.
- Established **measurement invariance** across sex and country on a multilingual sample (N = 1,420).
- Built an NLP classification pipeline reaching **AUC = 0.97**, and identified stylistic confounds inflating performance — reframed as a method-validation contribution.
