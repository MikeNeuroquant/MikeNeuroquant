Persona-Driven Bias in LLM-Simulated Therapists

Large Language Models are increasingly used as informal coaches and therapists. Their responses, though, tend to shift in stereotyped ways depending on how they are prompted and which persona they are asked to embody.

This project looks at what happens when an LLM is asked to impersonate a therapist. The goal is to investigate the biases that emerge from these personas, and to understand how those biases show up in the language and psychological state of the person on the other side of the conversation.

Approach

The work sits at the intersection of NLP, cognitive network science, and psychometrics, and draws on three complementary tools.

Transformer models pre-trained on mental-health text, such as MentalBERT, are used to characterize the clinical tone of each exchange. Explainable AI grounded in cognitive network science, including Textual Forma Mentis Networks and EmoAtlas, helps reveal the semantic and emotional structure underlying both the model's and the user's discourse. Established psychometric instruments like the DASS-21 and PANAS provide the anchor to validated clinical constructs.

More details will follow as the project develops.
