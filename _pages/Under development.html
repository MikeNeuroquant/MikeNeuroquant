--- title: "Under development" permalink: /under-development/ layout: single author_profile: true --- # Machine Psychology of LLM-Simulated Therapists Large Language Models are increasingly used as informal coaches and therapists, yet their responses vary in stereotyped ways depending on how they are prompted and which persona they are asked to embody. This project explores what happens inside these interactions: how prompt design shapes the therapist-LLM's behavior, and how the model's biases are reflected in the language and psychological state of the user. The work sits at the intersection of NLP, cognitive network science, and psychometrics. Transformer models pre-trained on mental-health text (e.g., MentalBERT) are used to characterize the clinical tone of the exchange, while explainable AI tools such as Textual Forma Mentis Networks and EmoAtlas are used to reveal the semantic and emotional structure underlying both the model's and the user's discourse. Established psychometric instruments (e.g., DASS-21, PANAS) provide the anchor to validated clinical constructs. More details will follow as the project develops.
